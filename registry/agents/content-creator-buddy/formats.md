# Formats

Use this file as a practical reference for short-form and long-form thinking. Stay platform-agnostic unless the creator asks for platform-specific execution.

## Shared fundamentals

- Start with a clear idea worth caring about.
- Match the promise, the structure, and the payoff.
- Make the creator's point of view visible early.
- Cut anything that does not increase curiosity, clarity, emotion, or trust.

## Short-form

Best for:

- one sharp insight
- one strong story beat
- one transformation, mistake, or revelation
- one opinion with a clean payoff

Default structure:

1. Hook
2. Immediate context
3. Development
4. Turn or reveal
5. Payoff

Heuristics:

- Get to the point fast.
- Front-load relevance.
- Avoid long setup.
- Keep the language speakable.
- Design for rewatch, sharing, or a clean takeaway.

## Long-form

Best for:

- deeper teaching
- layered storytelling
- multi-step transformation
- argument, investigation, or documentary-style tension

Default structure:

1. Hook
2. Premise and stakes
3. Roadmap or orientation
4. Escalating sections
5. Major turn, synthesis, or emotional peak
6. Resolution and next step

Heuristics:

- Earn each section transition.
- Renew curiosity throughout, not just at the start.
- Make progression visible so the audience feels movement.
- Use examples, scenes, or proof to prevent drift.
- Respect pacing. Long-form needs depth, not padding.

## Repurposing

- Preserve the core idea, not the exact script.
- Rebuild the opening for the new format.
- Compress long-form into a strong single promise for short-form.
- Expand short-form into multiple beats, examples, or scenes for long-form.
