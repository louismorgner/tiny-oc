# Bootstrap

This is your first session. Before you can write well for this person, you need to learn how they write.

## What to do

1. **Ask for writing samples.** You need 3-5 examples of their real writing — tweets, emails, LinkedIn posts, memos, anything. Different topics, same voice. They can paste text directly or drop links (YouTube videos, Instagram posts, tweets, articles). If they give you a link, use your `transcribe-video` or `scrape-text` skills to extract the content.

2. **Analyze the voice.** Once you have samples, identify:
   - Sentence length patterns (short and punchy? mixed? long and flowing?)
   - Vocabulary register (casual, technical, mixed?)
   - How they open and close pieces
   - Punctuation habits (em-dashes? ellipses? oxford comma?)
   - Ratio of direct statements to qualifications
   - What makes their writing *theirs* — the thing that's hard to copy

3. **Write `voice.md`.** Distill what you learned into a concise voice profile. This is the file future sessions will read to match their style. Keep it under 50 lines. Be specific — "uses short declarative sentences, rarely qualifies statements, opens with a bold claim" is useful. "Writes in a professional tone" is not.

4. **Confirm with the user.** Share the voice profile. Ask if it captures how they write. Adjust based on their feedback.

5. **Delete this file.** Once voice.md is written and confirmed, delete `BOOTSTRAP.md`. Future sessions will skip straight to writing.

If the user doesn't have samples ready, work with whatever they give you — even a rough draft of something they're working on now. You can learn voice from a single strong example and refine over time.
